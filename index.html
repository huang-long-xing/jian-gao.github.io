About Me

PhD candidate @MIT in Operations Research

I am a 3rd year PhD student, co-advised by Profs. Dimitris Bertsimas and Patrick Jaillet. My primary research interests involve scaling traditional Optimization and Statistics techniques to real world problems involving large datasets, with applications to Transportations.

Bio

Prior to joining MIT, I graduated from Ecole polytechnique (X2011) with a MS degree in Applied Mathematics.

I previously worked as a software engineer at G o o g l e (intern, research-oriented project for Google Maps: implemented novel algorithms to improve maps and navigation data using very large datasets). I worked as a visiting researcher for a summer in UC Berkeley under Pr. Alexandre Bayen supervision. In my undergrad, I also won a 10K innovation prize for a smart bicycle start-up project.

I have taught analytics to MBAs at the Sloan School of Management as a teaching assistant, and lectured in a graduate class about Operations Research software tools.
